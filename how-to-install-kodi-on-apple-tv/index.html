How to Install Kodi on Apple TV

First, make sure your Apple TV is on its latest software update. You’ll also need a Mac computer and a USB-C cable. Then, follow the steps below:

  1. Download and install Xcode on your Mac.
  2. Sign up for a developer account with Apple.
  3. Connect your Apple TV to your Mac using the USB-C cable.
  4. Open Xcode and click “Create a new Xcode Project.”
  5. Select “Application” under tvOS and “Single View App.”
  6. Enter a unique name for the app and a unique identifier, such as com.yourname.kodi.
  7. In the “Signing” section, select the Apple ID you signed up for the developer account with.
  8. Click “Fix Issue” if there is a signing issue and sign in with your Apple ID.
  9. On your Apple TV, go to “Settings” and select “Remotes and Devices.”
  10. Turn on “Remote App and Devices” and “Home Sharing.”
  11. On your Mac, click “Product” in Xcode and then “Destination.”
  12. Select “Apple TV” from the list of available devices.
  13. Click “Build and then Run.”
  14. Wait for the app to install on your Apple TV.
  15. Once the app is installed, launch it on your Apple TV and select “Add-ons.”
  16. Select “Install from repository” and choose your preferred add-ons.
  17. Enjoy streaming content using Kodi on your Apple TV!

Summary of How to Install Kodi on Apple TV
