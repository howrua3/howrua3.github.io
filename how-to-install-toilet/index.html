Answering the Target of How to Install Toilet

The first step in how to install a toilet is to gather the necessary tools and materials. You’ll need a new toilet, wax ring, mounting bolts, a wrench, a screwdriver, and a putty knife. Turn off your water supply valve and remove the old toilet by draining the tank and loosening the mounting bolts. From there, install the wax ring and mounting bolts on the flange and attach the new toilet bowl. Finally, connect the water supply line and secure the tank to the bowl.

How to Install Toilet: Setting the Toilet Bowl

Before starting, make sure the closet flange is level by measuring the height using a ruler or level. Once you have positioned the wax ring on the flange, gently place the toilet bowl on top of the wax ring, making sure it is aligned with the bolts on the floor. Press down firmly to anchor the bowl to the wax ring. Be careful not to rock the bowl back and forth to avoid damaging the wax ring.

Next, install the mounting bolts by aligning them with the holes on the base of the toilet bowl and tightening them with a wrench. Once the mounting bolts are secure, snap the bolt caps on top of them to cover the holes.

How to Install Toilet: Installing the Tank

You can now install the tank by positioning it on the back of the toilet bowl and lining it up with the mounting bolts. Tighten the nuts on both sides of the bolts, making sure not to over-tighten them. You can then attach the water supply line to the tank and tighten it with a wrench. After connecting the water supply, turn on the water valve and check for leaks. Finally, secure the lid on the tank and flush the toilet to make sure everything is working correctly.

### How to Install Toilet and Maintain It

Installing a toilet is just the first step. It’s important to maintain it properly to ensure it lasts for years to come. Regular cleaning and upkeep should be part of your plumbing routine. This includes cleaning the inside of the bowl and tank, checking for leaks, and fixing any minor issues that may arise. Remember to never flush anything other than toilet paper and human waste, as flushing anything else can cause blockages and damage your toilet.

#### Using a Toilet Auger for Clogs

If you have a stubborn clog that won’t budge, you can use a toilet auger to remove it. A toilet auger is a plumbing tool that is specifically designed for toilets. Simply insert the auger into the toilet bowl and push down on the handle to break up the clog. Once the clog is cleared, flush the toilet to confirm that the water is flowing properly.
